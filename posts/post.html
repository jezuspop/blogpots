Walking by Faith: Lessons from Hebrews 11

Discover what it truly means to live by faith as we explore the heroes of Hebrews 11 and learn how their trust in God can transform our daily walk.

RS
RS Buckner

There's a chapter in the Bible that reads like a hall of fame—not of athletes or entertainers, but of ordinary people who did extraordinary things because they believed God. Hebrews 11 is often called "The Faith Chapter," and for good reason. It gives us a front-row seat to the kind of faith that moves mountains, parts seas, and transforms lives.

But here's what strikes me most about this chapter: none of these heroes had it all figured out. They didn't have perfect circumstances. Many of them didn't even see the promises fulfilled in their lifetime. Yet they believed anyway.

"Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen."

— Hebrews 11:1 (KJV)

Understanding True Faith

Before we dive into the stories, we need to understand what faith actually is. The world often confuses faith with wishful thinking or blind optimism. But biblical faith is something entirely different.

The Greek word for "substance" here is hypostasis—it literally means the foundation or support structure beneath something. Faith isn't a feeling; it's the bedrock upon which we build our lives. When Abraham left Ur, he didn't have Google Maps or a five-year plan. He had a promise from God, and that promise became more real to him than the city walls he left behind.

1

Faith Has Substance

Faith isn't wishful thinking or positive vibes. It's the confident assurance that what God has promised, He will deliver. When you have faith, you're not hoping something might be true—you're standing on the certainty that it is true, even when you can't see it yet.

The Evidence of the Unseen

The second part of our key verse says faith is "the evidence of things not seen." Think about that word: evidence. In a courtroom, evidence is what convinces the jury. It's proof that holds up under cross-examination.

Moses is a perfect example. Hebrews 11:27 tells us he "endured as seeing him who is invisible." Moses saw Pharaoh's army, the Red Sea, the grumbling Israelites—all very visible problems. But he also saw something else: the invisible God who had called him. And that invisible reality was more convincing to him than what his eyes could see.

2

Faith Provides Evidence

Your faith becomes the proof of what you believe. When you step out in obedience before you see the outcome, when you give before you see provision, when you praise before you see victory—your actions become evidence that you truly believe God is who He says He is.

Seeing the Invisible

Every hero in Hebrews 11 had one thing in common: they saw something others couldn't. Noah saw rain before anyone knew what rain was. Abraham saw a nation when he had no children. Moses saw freedom when all Israel saw was slavery.

This is perhaps the most challenging aspect of faith. It requires us to value God's perspective more than our own. It asks us to trust His timeline when ours feels more urgent. It demands that we believe His promises when our circumstances scream something different.

"For we walk by faith, not by sight."

— 2 Corinthians 5:7 (ESV)
3

Faith Sees Beyond the Visible

Faith doesn't ignore reality—it sees a deeper reality. It recognizes that what God says is more certain than what our circumstances suggest. The challenge isn't to deny what we see, but to give greater weight to what God has spoken.

What Faith Looks Like Today

So what does this mean for us, living in the 21st century? We may not be parting seas or defeating giants, but we face our own battles that require the same kind of faith.

  • The diagnosis that threatens to steal your peace
  • The job loss that shakes your security
  • The relationship that seems beyond repair
  • The dream that feels impossible
  • The calling that terrifies you

In each of these situations, faith whispers the same truth it whispered to Abraham, Moses, and David: God is faithful. He keeps His promises. You can trust Him.

📝 This Week's Application

  • ✓ Read Hebrews 11 daily. Each day, choose one faith hero and journal about what they sacrificed and what they gained through faith.
  • ✓ Identify your "invisible." What is God asking you to believe for that you cannot yet see? Write it down and commit it to prayer.
  • ✓ Take one step of faith. Is there something God has been prompting you to do that you've been putting off? This week, take that step—not because you can see the outcome, but because you trust the One who does.
  • ✓ Share your story. Tell someone this week about a time God came through for you. Your testimony strengthens both your faith and theirs.

A Final Word

As we close, I want you to remember this: the heroes of Hebrews 11 weren't perfect people. They made mistakes. They had doubts. They sometimes failed spectacularly. But they're in this chapter because, despite everything, they kept believing.

The question isn't whether you'll have moments of doubt or seasons of struggle. The question is: Will you keep walking by faith even when the path is unclear?

My prayer for you this week is that you would experience the substance of faith in a fresh way—that God's promises would become so real to you that they outweigh any circumstance you face. May you walk by faith, not by sight, knowing that the God who called Abraham, who delivered Moses, and who empowered David is the same God who walks with you today.

"Let us run with endurance the race that is set before us, looking unto Jesus, the author and finisher of our faith."

— Hebrews 12:1-2 (NKJV)
