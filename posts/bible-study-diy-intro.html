Bible Study January 2025

Bible Study DIY 00: Introduction

Introducing our Regional Midweek series on personal Bible study—empowering you to engage deeply with Scripture and transform your perspective in the sanctuary of God.

The "DIY" series emphasizes that we have the ability to have great Bible study. This isn't about advocating self-reliance, but rather empowering personal engagement with Scripture. When we learn to study God's Word effectively, something transformative happens.

"But then one day I was brought into the sanctuaries of God, and in the light of glory, my distorted perspective vanished."

— Psalm 73:17 (TPT)

Our fight is to get into the sanctuary. It's like being transported into another dimension—that is what good Bible study and connection with God does. It shifts everything.

How Would You Rate Your Bible Study?

Before we dive into this series, take a moment to honestly assess where you are:

Self-Assessment

  • Going great; need more of a challenge What's the next level for you?
  • Going ok; need a clear focus You need something specific to study
  • Not going well; need help Feeling inconsistent and uninspired
  • Non-existent; don't know where to start You need someone to sit down and show you

Wherever you find yourself, this series is designed to meet you there and help you grow.

1

The Desire to Pursue

The Bible truly emphasizes that a pursuit of God should be a life-giving, faith-filled action that fosters a deep relationship. Consider what Scripture tells us:

  • Ecclesiastes 3:11 — God has set eternity into the hearts of men. Humanity was created for a relationship with God.
  • John 10:10 — Satan wants to destroy all the good God created. We must be careful not to allow luxuries to distract or hinder our pursuit of God.
  • Psalm 34:4 — When we seek the Lord, He answers us. He delivers us from all our fears.
  • Jeremiah 29:13 — God's pursuit requires wholehearted searching. We find Him when we seek Him fully.
2

Become Like Children

Jesus gives us a powerful picture of the posture we need when approaching Scripture and our relationship with God:

"Jesus called a little child to come to him. He stood the child in front of the followers. Then he said, 'The truth is, you must change your thinking and become like little children. If you don't do this, you will never enter God's kingdom.'"

— Matthew 18:2-3 (ERV)

What childlike qualities should we embrace as we approach Bible study?

  • Humility — Coming with an open, teachable spirit
  • Trust — Believing what God says is true
  • Forgiveness — Letting go of past hurts and offenses
  • Purity — Approaching with clean motives
  • Joy — Finding delight in discovery
  • Wonder — Being amazed by God's Word
  • Fascination — Captivated by the stories and truths
  • Curiosity — Always asking questions and wanting to learn more
